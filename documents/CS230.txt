Spring 2020 - COMPSCI 230 - Discrete Mathematics for Computer Science
Syllabus

Course Overview
Discrete mathematics lays the foundation on which much of modern computer science rests. This course provides an undergraduate-level introduction to discrete mathematics, highlighting its relevance to computer science through mathematical logic, set theory, mathematical induction, combinatorics, probability, and graph theory.

3 Course Content & Resources

Core Synopsis of Topics
1. Introduction: Proof techniques
2. Mathematical Logic: Propositional/Predicate Logic, Gödel's Incompleteness Theorem
3. Set Theory: Sets, Sequences, Relations, Functions, Partial and Total Orders
4. Mathematical Induction: Weak, Strong, and Structural Induction
5. Graph Theory: Graph properties, Acyclic graphs (DAGs, Trees, Forests), Matchings
6. Counting: Finite/Infinite Sets, Countable and Uncountable Sets
7. Probability: Sample Spaces, Conditional Probability, Independence, Random Variables, Distributions

Course Text & Materials

Homework & Submission Guidelines
* Submission Platform: Gradescope is used for all written and programming homework assignments.
* Formatting: Written solutions must be cleanly typed and uploaded in PDF format. Formatting via LaTeX is highly encouraged. All programming solutions are evaluated via Gradescope frameworks.
* Late Penalties: Homework is due strictly at 11:59pm Eastern Time.
  * Submissions between 12:00am and 12:29am encounter an automatic 10% penalty deduction.
  * Submissions from 12:30am onward are rejected automatically for 0 credit.
  * Technical Backup: If Gradescope server issues occur, you may email files directly to course staff before the deadline. The same timestamp rules and late windows apply.
* Extensions: Formal extensions must be requested by emailing the instructor directly *before* the submission deadline with an explicit justification. Granting extensions remains at the instructor's discretion; last-minute requests run the risk of denial, leaving the original deadline standing. TAs are unauthorized to modify deadlines.
* Collaboration: Review the detailed course collaboration guidelines. Violations of the academic honesty policy are passed directly to university disciplinary boards without exception.
