2 Course Details

Topics Covered
* Python Data, Variables, operators
* Functions
* Conditionals (if), logic operators
* Looping structures (for, while)
* Turtle Graphics
* Strings
* Lists, Tuples
* Files
* Sets, Dictionaries
* Recursion

Learning Goals
1. Problem Decomposition: Dissect problems into manageable smaller units, code solutions for these units, and integrate these solutions to address the overarching goal.
2. Python Proficiency: Develop your own Python program, be it a game, data analysis tool, or a creative venture. This encompasses fundamental constructs, idioms, basic data structures, and introductory testing methods.
3. Library Literacy: Dive into Python library documentation outside our course curriculum and understand them enough to use some of their functionality.

4 Policies & Grading

Grading Breakdown
Grading is on an absolute scale (no curve): 94%+=A, 90%=A-, 80%=B range, 70%=C range, 60%=D.

Category                                | Weight
----------------------------------------|--------
Labs                                    | 6%
Reading Quizzes (PrairieLearn)           | 2%
Class Participation (WOTOs)             | 2%
APTs                                    | 10%
Programming Assignments                | 10%
Three Midterm Exams (15% each) & Final  | 70% (Midterms 45% total, Final 25%)

AI (LLM) Policy
* Prohibited: You cannot use AI/LLMs to solve problems or write code for you.
* Allowed: You may use LLMs for general foundational questions (e.g., "What is the format of an if statement in Python?", "Give me an example of a list loop for a beginner").

Pre-Lecture Work & Quizzes
* Readings/videos are assigned before each lecture. 
* A corresponding PrairieLearn quiz must be submitted BEFORE 10:00am on the day of lecture. No makeups allowed. 
* Up to 3 attempts are allowed per quiz (highest score counts). Lowest quiz points dropped at the end of the semester.
* Exception: QZ01-QZ05 will remain open until Sept. 9 at 10:00am to accommodate drop/add.
* Programming assignments also have a dedicated reading quiz in PrairieLearn to be completed before coding.

Lecture & WOTOs
* Attendance is required for your assigned section. 
* Lectures include synchronous WOTO (working together) peer activities in the textbook that must be completed during class. A few low WOTO scores are dropped.

Labs
* Held on Fridays. Attendance is mandatory and recorded by UTAs.
* Completed with a partner, but individual forms must be submitted by Sunday 11:59pm following the lab.
* Grading (5-point scale): 1 pt pre-lab, 2 pts attendance, 2 pts completion. (If no pre-lab, 3 pts completion).
* Absences: If you miss lab, you can submit the form by Sunday for partial credit (losing the 2 attendance points). Missing more than 3 labs will impact your grade; the 3 lowest labs are dropped, meaning attendance points cannot be recovered for any reason.

APTs
* Code solutions are evaluated purely via automated online tests. Hardcoded input strings checking via sequential if-statements receive 0 credit.
* Due at 11:59pm on due dates. A 24-hour grace period applies automatically (no penalty). Late submissions past the grace period are not accepted.

Programming Assignments
* Due at 11:59pm. Submissions go through Gradescope.
* Passing 85% of automated test suites typically results in full credit. If you suspect a Gradescope framework bug, verify it with a Grad TA/Instructor on Ed or in OH before grading reconciliation.
* Late Policy:
  * Up to 24 hours late: Grace period (0% penalty)
  * 24 to 72 hours late: 10% penalty
  * 72 hours to 1 week late: 30% penalty
  * Post 1 week: No submissions accepted.

Extensions
* Every student receives four free 3-day extensions to use on any APT or assignment (except during the final week of classes). 
* A 3-day extension means 2 days past the standard grace period. You must complete the extension form on the course site before the original deadline. Only 1 extension permitted per assignment.
* Extensions are also granted for documented athletic travel or medical reasons (requires filling out both the course extension form and the Duke Short-term Incapacitation Notification).

Exams
* Three midterms and one comprehensive Block Final Exam. 
* Final Exam Date: Thursday, December 11, 7:00pm - 10:00pm (Room TBA).
* Final Score Adjustment: At the end of the term, if your final exam grade is higher than your lowest midterm grade, the difference (up to a maximum of 15 points) will be added to that lowest midterm score.
* Missed Midterms: Excused absences (e.g., Short-Term Incapacitation) must be made up within 3 class days. Unexcused misses score 0.
* Rules: Exams are individual, closed-communication work. You cannot discuss an exam with anyone until it is formally handed back.
